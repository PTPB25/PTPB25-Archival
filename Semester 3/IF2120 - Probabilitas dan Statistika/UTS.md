1. A wildlife monitoring system uses motion-activated cameras that independently detect animal movement with a success probability of `0.8`. If the system observes 12 areas during one night:
   1. What is the probability that **exactly 9 areas** successfully detect animal movement?
   2. What is the probability that **at least 10 areas** successfully detect animal movement?

2. A digital sound classification model categorizes audio clips into 3 categories: Music, Speech, and Noise. From the training data, the classification probabilities for each clip are as follows:
    * Music: p₁ = `0.5`
    * Speech: p₂ = `0.3`
    * Noise: p₃ = `0.2`
  
   If the model is used to classify 10 audio clips independently:
   1. What is the probability that exactly **4 clips** are classified as **Music**, **3 as Speech**, and **3 as Noise**?
   2. What is the probability that exactly **6 clips** are **not classified as Music**?

3. A warehouse stores 50 electronic components, of which 5 are defective. A quality inspector randomly selects 7 components for inspection without replacement.
   1. What is the probability that **exactly 2 defective** components are selected in the sample?
   2. What is the probability that **at least 1 defective** component is selected?

4. A data center uses specialized cooling units to maintain optimal server temperature. If a cooling unit fails, maintenance must be done, and no new data can be processed that day. The probability that a given day becomes a cooling maintenance day is `0.45`. The data center operates 6 days a week (Monday to Saturday).
   1. If the **first day is Monday**, calculate the probability that the **first cooling maintenance day** occurs on the **first Wednesday**. 
   2. Within a period of **5 days**, calculate the probability that the **third cooling maintenance day** occurs by the end of the period.

5. The Transportation Department is evaluating whether to deploy a roadside assistance unit on a certain road to quickly handle vehicle breakdowns during the evening rush hour. This road is used by over 7,000 vehicles during peak hours. Records show that the average number of breakdown incidents per evening during peak hours is `3`. <br>
The department will only deploy the assistance unit if the probability of more than 3 incidents occurring during an evening is at least `30%`.
Based on this information, **should the Transportation Department deploy the unit?**

6. BookBazaar.com is an online bookstore that organizes seasonal sales where customers often purchase multiple items in a single transaction. The company analyzed its customer data to determine how much people spend per order during these sales. The study revealed that the spending is approximately normally distributed with a mean of `$4.11` and a standard deviation of `$1.37`.
   1. What percentage of customers spend **less than $3** during the sale? 
   2. What is **the minimum amount** a customer must spend to be in the top `87%` of all spenders?
   
7. The serum cholesterol level of 16-year-old students is approximately normally distributed with a mean of `170 mg/dL` and a standard deviation of `30 mg/dL`.
   1. What is the probability that a randomly selected student has serum cholesterol level greater than `230 mg/dL`?
   2. At a high school, there are `250` students aged 16. What is the probability that at least `5` of them have serum cholesterol level greater than `230 mg/dL`?

8. In an ecological study, the length of time (in months) that a certain species of fish survives in a polluted river follows a gamma distribution with parameters `α = 5` and `β = 10`.

   1. What is **the average** survival time of a randomly selected fish from this species?

   2. What is **the standard deviation** of the survival time?

   3. What is the probability that a fish survives more than `30 months`?

9. The time (in hours) a technician takes to repair a computer follows an exponential distribution with an average repair time of 4 hours. What is the probability that the technician completes the repair in **less than 3 hours** on at least **4 out of the next 6 jobs**?
